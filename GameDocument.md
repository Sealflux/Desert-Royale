# Core Mechanics


What does a single turn look like?


A player gets two Action points at the beginning of their turn. Each player has two Action Cards which they can use their Action points on if they want to. If they use all of their Action points, then their turn is done. If they used an Action card, after their turn ends, they can pick up Action Cards until they have two again. The player can turn their head in any direction which takes one Action point. The player can move forward one in the direction that their head is facing but only in the four cardinal directions, not moving diagonally. The player can shoot 3 spaces in front of them or 1 space diagonally in front of them which uses up one action point along with a bullet. The player can spend one Action point reloading their storage of 3 bullets.


How do you win?


The last player standing will win the game. Each player starts with two Action Cards along with their 3 bullets fully loaded along with 3 Health Points. You cannot regain Health Points naturally unless done through an Action Card. Once all 3 Health Points are depleted, you are out of the game.


How does someone get eliminated?


Each player has 3 Health Points and whenever you take damage, whether that be through someone's use of Action Cards or shooting a bullet at your face, you will lose that corresponding amount of health in damage.


How many players?


I've personally played with 5 others, including myself. That's a group of 6 people playing, and it was quite fun. The minimum would be 2 players and I don't think there's a maximum possible.


Roughly how long does one game take in person?


One game took around 30-60 minutes. However the online version will include turn timers so games don't take that long.


# The Board & Pieces


What does the board look like?


The board is a grid of squares and within this grid of squares, there are specific tiles in which the players can move. Imagine a grass field and in this grass field, there's 1 by 1 meter concrete square blocks that the players move on. Between each concrete square block, players can place two types of walls, a wall that blocks movement but doesn't protect you from getting shot and a wall that blocks both bullets and movement.


How big is the board?


I currently don't know how big the board is, but I assume it can be expanded or shrinked depending on player size.


What pieces are on the board?


There are three walls per player that are placed initially when the game starts. There is the representation of the player on the board as well. That's about it for pieces that are ON the board. Outside of the board, there are piercing bullets along with shotgun bullets that are placed in a player's ammo belt through Action Cards.

What resources do players manage?


Players have to manage their limited supply of Action Points(2) per turn along with managing their ammo belt as they need to remember to refill/resupply their bullets. Players also have to consider their Health Points in combat encounters.


How do you gain or lose resources?


Players gain 2 Action Points when their turn begins and use them in actions whether that be shooting someone or using an Action Card. Players lose one bullet from their ammo belt of 3 bullets whenever a player uses the action of shooting. A player replenishes their Action Cards(2) whenever their turn ends and they have less than 2, back up to 2 Action Cards.


# Combat & Interaction


How do players interact?


Players interact through combat or a combat-oriented Action Card.


How is combat resolved?


You are either within range or not within range of getting shot, if you are in range and you get shot, you immediately lose the amount of health points corresponding to the amount of damage you take.


Can players form alliances?


Players are free to team however, they can also easily betray each other so be careful of who you trust in Desert Royale.


What happens when two players land on the same space?


Players can not push each other off a space. If they happen to use an Action Card that makes them land on the same space, they will simply be pushed to the space where their head is facing and if that space is obstructed, then the next space in front of their head and so forth.


# Hidden Information


What do other players not know?


Players don't know each other's hand which consists of 2 Action Cards. Anything else is known. Whether it is your position on the board, your health, and how many bullets are on your ammo belt.

# Edge Cases


What happens when you run out of cards in the draw pile?


The discard pile/ the pile of used cards will be shuffled and become the new draw pile.


What if a player has no valid moves?


A player always has the option to turn their head, load bullets into their ammo belt(3) max of 3 bullets and use their Action Cards. If a player becomes boxed by walls, they are safe but are also trapped, they can get out using Action Cards eventually.
